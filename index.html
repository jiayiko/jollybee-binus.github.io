CP
Competitive Programming, or CP in short, is a mind sport in which contestants are given a set of programming problems to be solved within a preset amount of time. Competitions are supported by various companies such as Huawei, Google, Facebook, Shopee, Agoda, Acer, Telegram, Traveloka, Sirclo, and others.
Jollybee
Jollybee is Bina Nusantara University's official CP team. Currently, Jollybee has 30+ active members being coached directly by the former chief judge of the Indonesian National Science Olympiad in Informatics (OSN bidang Komputer) 2020 and former judge of the International Olympiad in Informatics (IOI) 2022.
Jollybee has won numerous national and international competitions and has represented Indonesia in the ICPC World Finals several times.
Please visit our Instagram or Linkedin to view our latest achievements

.. of learning CP:
  • Be proficient at algorithms and data structures
  • Improve your problem solving skills
  • Improve your general coding skills (implementation, debugging, etc.)
  • Learn to think effectively and efficiently
  • Learn to code and solve problems as part of a team
  • Get industry recognition
  • Win achievements for your CV

.. of joining Jollybee:
  • Take part in regular CP Training sessions
  • Be part of a solid community with a shared interest in programming

  • Join alumni sharing sessions
  • Visit tech companies
  • All of this for free! (all competition-related expenses are paid by Binus University)
Our Alumni
Competitive Programming has shaped the mindset of our members, refining them into the best version of themselves. Due to their own efforts, some of the companies of our recent alumni are Shopee, Google, Amazon, Meta, Grab, Tiket.com, Traveloka, Tokopedia, Sirclo, and others.
Some of our alumni also choose to thrive in education and research as a coach, a lecturer, or a researcher in educational institutes such as the National University of Singapore, University of Tsukuba, and Binus University.

Your starting point
There are various ways to start your CP journey.
For newcomers with no coding experience, we recommend learning from the TLX basic programming courses prepared by TOKI.
Already have coding experience? We recommend you to try their basic CP courses for more challenging topics.

Spice it up with Online Judges
Participate in live online contests. We suggest trying Atcoder Beginner Contest, Codeforces Div. 4, and Codeforces Div. 3 for starters to experience how it feels to be in a real CP competition. We don't recommend doing this before you've finished TLX's introductory courses.
Our Online Judge recommendations
  • Codeforces
  • Atcoder
  • TLX
Some things to remember..
If you can't solve any problems in a contest, don't be discouraged. Try our recommended courses above and practice some more.

Everyone started out as a newbie; it's just that you've only just taken your first step in CP. Don't worry, our selection phases are designed for starters in basic programming.
One last thing
Practice, practice, practice
Please watch How to start Competitive Programming? For beginners! by Errichto.

If you keep Errichto's advice in mind and practice diligently, you should be able to improve fast.
